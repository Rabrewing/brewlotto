# BrewLotto V1 Launch Marketing Roadmap

## Purpose

This checklist is the working marketing plan for BrewLotto V1.

Use it when V1 is ready to launch publicly, then revisit it weekly until the funnel is stable.

## Product Positioning

### Core message

BrewLotto helps players:

- save smarter picks
- track saved hits in My Picks
- confirm plays cleanly
- understand results by state and draw window
- unlock deeper timing analysis with TimePulse and TimePulse II

### What BrewLotto is not

- not a generic lottery ticket seller
- not a “get rich quick” app
- not a broad national lottery marketplace
- not a copy of Jackpocket or Lotto.com

### Short positioning statement

`BrewLotto helps NC and CA players save smarter picks, track hits, and learn their best play patterns by state, game, and draw window.`

## Success Definition

The launch is working if the app converts in this order:

1. landing page visit
2. sign-in or magic link
3. first saved pick
4. first saved hit shown in My Picks
5. first confirmed play
6. first result / win / Fireball update
7. first upgrade or renewed session

If users do not reach step 3 quickly, improve onboarding and store pages before buying traffic.

## Phase 0 - Foundation Before Launch

- [ ] Confirm V1 feature scope is frozen
- [ ] Confirm the landing page, onboarding, dashboard, Strategy Locker, My Picks, Results, Stats, Billing, BrewU, Support, and QA/Test Lab are stable
- [ ] Draft and review the BrewLotto legal policy pack: Terms of Use, Privacy Policy, California supplement, AI Usage Disclosure, Intellectual Property / Internet Property, and Responsible Play disclaimer
- [x] Add a dedicated privacy request path and confirm the California privacy addendum has its own route
- [ ] Keep the referral loop deferred until billing, notifications, and strategy gating are stable
- [ ] Verify the current tier ladder copy is final
- [ ] Verify the Fireball copy is final for NC Pick 3 and Pick 4
- [ ] Verify TimePulse is shown for BrewPro and TimePulse II for BrewMaster
- [ ] Verify trial banner wording and upgrade prompts are final
- [ ] Verify support intake and screenshot flow work end to end
- [ ] Verify the app has no obvious responsive layout regressions on desktop, tablet, and mobile

### Guidance notes

- Do not start paid acquisition until the first-value loop is working.
- The first-value loop is: sign in -> save pick -> see hit -> confirm play -> see result.
- If any of those steps feel confusing, fix the product before trying to scale traffic.
- Referral is a V1.5 item, not a launch dependency. Do not block V1 on it.

## Phase 1 - App Store / Storefront Readiness

### Apple App Store readiness

- [ ] Prepare App Store screenshots in the right order
- [ ] Prepare the preview video
- [ ] Write the title and subtitle
- [ ] Write the keyword field
- [ ] Prepare the App Review notes
- [ ] Decide if custom product pages are needed for different audiences

### Google Play readiness

- [ ] Prepare Play Store screenshots
- [ ] Write the short description
- [ ] Write the full description
- [ ] Prepare the feature graphic
- [ ] Prepare the promo video if needed
- [ ] Check policy compliance for lottery/gambling-adjacent wording

### Guidance notes

- Lead with saved hits, strategy tracking, and educational value.
- Avoid positioning the app as a generic ticket-buying app if that is not the real product.
- Keep the first screenshot about the core value, not the logo.
- Keep the second screenshot about My Picks or Strategy Locker.
- Keep the third screenshot about TimePulse / TimePulse II or Results.

### Store asset order

1. Hero screenshot: landing/video + main product promise
2. Saved hit screenshot: My Picks
3. Strategy screenshot: Strategy Locker
4. Results screenshot: draw matching and Fireball
5. Stats screenshot: performance summary
6. Help screenshot: BrewU

## Phase 2 - Launch Week Checklist

- [ ] Announce the launch to the tester group
- [ ] Send the short group text with the app link
- [ ] Ask testers to use incognito/private mode if they were previously using the app
- [ ] Give testers a 7-day testing window
- [ ] Ask testers to complete the guided pass first
- [ ] Ask testers to complete the free-roam pass second
- [ ] Ask testers to capture screenshots when something feels unclear
- [ ] Ask testers to verify TimePulse shows for BrewPro and TimePulse II shows for BrewMaster in Strategy Locker, My Picks, and Stats
- [ ] Watch install-to-save conversion
- [ ] Watch saved-hit visibility in My Picks
- [ ] Watch confirmed-play flow and Fireball flow
- [ ] Watch upgrade/downgrade flow in Billing
- [ ] Watch support submissions and screenshot uploads

### Launch week guidance notes

- Do not ask testers to be vague.
- Ask them to report:
  - what they clicked
  - what they expected
  - what actually happened
  - whether they needed help
- If they get lost, that is a product cue issue, not just a bug.

### Test Lab hardening follow-up

These are the QA gaps that should stay visible after the guided/free-roam pass:

- [ ] Add a BrewCommand `/admin/qa` dashboard so QA reports can be browsed in one place over time
- [ ] Add CA-specific guided coverage for Daily 3, Daily 4, and Fantasy 5
- [ ] Add a freshness pre-check step so testers confirm the game is healthy before they run a flow
- [ ] Add explicit match-chip / hit-vs-win verification in My Picks and Stats
- [ ] Add TimePulse refresh / cooldown verification to the guided flow
- [ ] Add My Picks draw-window filter verification for Pick 3 / Pick 4 and confirm the filter stays hidden for Cash 5 / Powerball
- [ ] Keep these items tracked as QA hardening follow-ups, not launch blockers, unless they expose a real product break

## KPI Targets

Track these numbers every week:

- landing page click-through rate
- sign-in completion rate
- first saved pick rate
- My Picks revisit rate
- confirmed-play rate
- upgrade conversion rate
- support ticket rate per active user

### Practical targets to aim for

- At least 60 percent of new testers should reach a first saved pick
- At least 40 percent should confirm at least one play
- At least 25 percent should revisit My Picks after the first session
- At least 10 percent should interact with BrewU or Support

These are starting targets, not guarantees. Adjust them once real data comes in.

## Content Plan

Publish content that explains the product:

- What is TimePulse?
- What is TimePulse II?
- What does saved hit mean?
- How does Fireball work in NC?
- Why do Midday and Evening matter?
- How do I use Strategy Locker?
- What should I check in My Picks?
- How do I know if a result is a win?
- Where do TimePulse and TimePulse II show up in the app?

### Content guidance notes

- Teach before you sell.
- Explain the mechanics before you ask for a subscription.
- Show screenshots in the content when possible.
- Keep the language simple enough for a first-time lottery app user.
- Keep TimePulse explanations tied to BrewPro and BrewMaster so testers know which tier should show which timing view.

## Acquisition Channels

### Best early channels

- [ ] Existing tester group
- [ ] Direct sharing by text message
- [ ] Organic social posts
- [ ] Reddit or community posts, if policy-compliant
- [ ] BrewU educational content
- [ ] App store search

### Paid channels

- [ ] Only after the funnel converts
- [ ] Only after store pages are polished
- [ ] Only after the landing page has a clear conversion path

### Guidance notes

- Do not spend on ads first.
- Spend after the app proves it can convert.
- If the organic funnel is weak, ads will usually just make the weakness more expensive.

## Weekly Operating Loop

Every week after launch:

1. Review installs
2. Review saves
3. Review confirmed plays
4. Review wins / Fireball updates
5. Review upgrades and downgrades
6. Review support tickets
7. Update screenshots or copy if users are confused
8. Add one new content asset
9. Re-test the weakest funnel step

## Do Not Do Yet

- do not buy traffic before conversion is proven
- do not change the tier names casually
- do not collapse saved hits and confirmed wins into one metric
- do not market Fireball as a general feature outside NC Pick 3 / Pick 4
- do not use generic lottery-app messaging
- do not add referral loops before the core funnel is stable

## Notes for Future Revisions

When we revisit this roadmap, update:

- screenshots
- launch messaging
- store copy
- KPI targets
- channel priority
- tester instructions

If a launch step repeatedly confuses users, it becomes a product fix first and a marketing note second.
